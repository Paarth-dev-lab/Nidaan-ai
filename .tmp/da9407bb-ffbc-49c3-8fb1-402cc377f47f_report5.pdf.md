## Overview
The patient, Ms. Nirmala Goswami, a 65-year-old female, has undergone a series of lab tests to assess her overall health. The results indicate some abnormalities in her kidney function and inflammatory markers.

- Elevated ESR (Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate) at 83, indicating inflammation.
- Elevated Creatinine levels at 1.68, suggesting impaired kidney function.
- Urea levels are within the reference range but on the higher end.
## Future Risks
If left unaddressed, impaired kidney function and chronic inflammation can lead to more severe conditions, including kidney disease and cardiovascular issues.

Regular monitoring of kidney function and addressing the underlying cause of inflammation are crucial. Dietary adjustments and possibly medication may be necessary to manage these conditions.
